Segment 3 · Play 1
Stage 2 Trigger
The Re-Engagement Play
Trigger signals
Login frequency declining (30-day window) · Support tickets unresolved >14 days · NPS drop ≥15 points. Any one = assessment. Two or more = mandatory.
Execution
Executive-level CSM call — specific stated purpose: "I want to make sure we're solving the right problems for you."
Discipline
Listen:answer ratio must be 3:1. Talking >25% of the time = you've turned it into a pitch.
What it isn't
A check-in disguised as care. Customers feel the difference. Run the genuine version.
Segment 3 · Play 2
Stage 2–3 Trigger
The Value Recalibration Play
Trigger signals
Success plan not referenced 60+ days · Vague ROI answers when asked · Usage stable but no growth · Champion no longer present on calls
Execution
Pull every documented outcome since start. Rebuild value case from their stated objectives. Present: "Here is what we've delivered. Here is what you told us mattered. Here is the gap. Here is the plan to close it."
What it isn't
A victory lap. The customer knows what's working. They need you to acknowledge what isn't — and show a credible path forward.
Segment 4 · Play 3
Stage 4 Trigger — Champion Departure
The Champion Recovery Play
Context
Champion departures preceded 40% of churns. This play is mandatory when primary champion leaves — not optional.
Component 1
Stakeholder mapping within 72 hours. Who now owns the budget? Who owns the outcome? Often two different people.
Component 2
Establish relationship before renewal cycle. Waiting until 60 days before renewal = no relationship capital = vendor asking a stranger for a renewal.
Component 3
Abbreviated 45-min onboarding. Give them context to be your champion before you need them to be: what we've built, why it matters, how we move forward.
Segment 4 · Play 4
Stage 4–5 Trigger — Last Viable Intervention
The Executive Escalation Play
Trigger
CSM-level relationship is insufficient. Customer in active competitive evaluation. Economic buyer no longer accessible through CSM channel.
Execution
Executive-to-executive engagement. Your executive sponsor reaches out directly to their economic buyer.
The framing
"I want to make sure we understand what you need to make this decision fairly, and that you have everything from us to make it."
NOT: "I want to save this account." That framing kills the play.
Success rate
15–20%. Not low when the alternative is 12 months of ARR in replacement acquisition cost. Run it. Accept the odds.
Segment 5 · Building the System

From Plays to System

Four plays are tactics. A system is what makes them fire automatically — at the right moment, for the right account, without requiring a CSM to notice a problem first.

1
Signal Detection
Specific numerical thresholds — not "declining usage" but "login frequency below 40% of 30-day baseline for 14 consecutive days." Ambiguous thresholds don't fire. They get debated.
2
Play Assignment Protocol
Documented rules, not CSM judgment. Stage 2 signals → Play 1 assessment within 24 hours. Champion departure → Play 3 protocol within 72 hours. No exceptions. No "wait and see."
3
Escalation Authority
Executives pre-committed before crisis — not during it. When Play 4 fires, they respond in 48 hours. Get their agreement now. Trying during an active Stage 5 costs you 48 hours you don't have.

  • 3
    Define your Play 1 trigger thresholds today. Login frequency: what number? NPS drop: what point value? Tickets: how many days? Three specific numbers. Not descriptions. If you can't define the threshold, the system will never fire automatically.
