The Structural Distinction

Signal-Triggered
vs. Calendar-Triggered

Signal-Triggered ✓
Something Happened
Usage dropped. A milestone was hit. Support velocity spiked. Renewal is T-180. Champion change detected. There is a reason, and the customer can see it.
Calendar-Triggered ⚠
It's Thursday
The recurring cadence says to reach out. No specific reason. The customer shows up, nothing meaningful happens, and leaves having spent an hour that added no value to their business.
Calendar-triggered touchpoints with no purpose accumulate into a customer who sees your engagement as an obligation, not an asset.

Type 01
01
PURPOSELESS CHECK-IN
What the Customer Hears
"You don't know what's important to me. You don't have a specific reason to reach out. You're using me to fulfill a cadence requirement."
The Fix
Before any outreach, answer: what specific value will the customer take away? A usage insight, benchmark, industry update, or next-step milestone are all reasons. "Hi, checking in" is not.

Types 02 & 03
02
FEATURE DUMP
Customer Hears
"Figure out which features matter to you on your own."
Fix
Translate every announcement to this customer's specific goals. Four minutes of research. The difference is being known vs. being on a list.
03
REPETITIVE ASK
Customer Hears
"This relationship isn't important enough for you to remember what I've told you."
Fix
Everything into the CRM immediately. Review before every call. Three minutes. Non-negotiable.

Types 04 & 05 — The High-Stakes Ones
04
ESCALATION AMBUSH
Customer Hears
"I'm a revenue line, not a relationship. My CSM can't give me real information."
Fix
Advocate internally for proactive communication. Be first with information — or first with context and a plan. Second with news, first with clarity is still valuable.
05
HOLLOW BUSINESS REVIEW
Customer Hears
"This review was about your metrics, not my outcomes. You showed me what you tracked."
Fix
Open with their goals. Show progress against those goals. Close with what's next. Data serves the story — it doesn't replace it.

The Practical Filter

The Touchpoint Test

STEP 1
Signal-triggered or calendar-triggered?
Something specific happened → you're on solid ground. It's on the calendar → proceed to Step 2.
STEP 2
What specific value does this deliver, right now?
Answer it concretely → the touchpoint is justified. Can't answer it concretely → do not send.
STEP 3
No clear value? Reschedule or repurpose first.
Don't cancel — upgrade. Give it a reason. Every interaction the customer has with you should be something they'd describe as useful.
Not frequency. Quality. Accounts where every touchpoint has a reason are accounts where customers say "my CSM always has something valuable to share."

The Asymmetry That Changes Everything

Customers Remember
Friction Longer Than Value

Your customers don't remember every good thing you've done. But they remember every time you wasted their time. Negative touchpoints accumulate in customer memory in a way that positive ones often don't.
The only way to counteract the asymmetry: make every interaction earn its place. Not occasionally. Every time.
