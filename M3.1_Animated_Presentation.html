Moment 01
01
THE ADOPTION CLIFF
Window: Weeks 3–8 post go-live · The most commonly missed inflection point
Warning Signals
  • Usage frequency declining after initial peak
  • Gap between seats purchased and seats active
  • Users who logged in week 1 and haven't since
  • Champion re-focused on other priorities
Your Response
  • Structured adoption review call — not a check-in email
  • Confirm priority use case from discovery notes
  • Surface adoption blockers explicitly
  • Act before week 10 — behavioral patterns form fast

Moment 02
02
CHAMPION CHANGE
Window: Any time · Detect it early — the signals are there before the email
Early Signals
  • LinkedIn activity: "open to work," profile updates
  • Engagement drop — hours to days response time
  • Repeated missed meetings or sudden conflicts
  • Reduced energy or advocacy in calls
Your Response
  • Quietly accelerate multi-threading — not interrogate
  • Build sponsor relationship now, before the transition
  • Strengthen senior user relationships
  • See Module 2.2 for the 48-Hour Protocol post-departure

Moment 03
03
SUPPORT ESCALATION VELOCITY
Window: Ongoing · Not about one ticket — the pattern is the signal
Warning Signals
  • Volume spike: 1/mo for months → 5 in two weeks
  • Resolution time rising: one-touch → multi-contact
  • Tone shift: polite inquiry → "this is unacceptable"
  • Repeated issues with same root cause
Your Response
  • Review Tier 1 support activity weekly, Tier 2 monthly
  • Proactive outreach before they escalate to your VP
  • "I noticed a few recent tickets — nothing larger going on?"
  • This converts potential detractors into advocates

Moments 04 & 05 — The Opportunity Windows

Not Warning Signs.
Windows.

04
EXPANSION WINDOW
Opens: T-90 to T-120 before their budget cycle
Every company has budget cycles. If you don't know when theirs is, you'll arrive with a proposal after the money is already allocated.

Know their fiscal year end, their planning timeline, and any internal initiatives that create expansion opportunity. The window closes fast.
05
RENEWAL GRAVITY WELL
Starts: T-180 (6 months before renewal)
A renewal is not a transaction. It's the outcome of the last six months. If you start at T-90, you're reacting to a trajectory already set.

At T-180: sponsor check-in, health score review, begin value inventory. Build the story before the conversation starts.

Moment 04 — Deep Dive

The Expansion Window
Opens 90–120 Days Early

The customer decides in November. The CSM comes with a proposal in January. The opportunity is gone. You cannot position expansion without knowing when the money gets allocated.
For every Tier 1 account: know their fiscal year end, their annual planning timeline, and the internal initiatives that could create a natural expansion conversation.

Moment 05 — Deep Dive

T-180: Before the Trajectory
Is Already Set

A renewal is not a transaction — it's the outcome of everything that happened in the prior six months. If you start at T-90, you're reacting. Start at T-180 and you can still change the story.
T-180 action: Sponsor check-in on business outcomes · Health score review to surface what needs fixing · Begin your value inventory — the documented evidence of what changed because this customer chose you.
