Segment 1 · The Heresy

"Digital-First for Every
Customer. Including Enterprise."

Every time I present this, approximately half the room looks like I've insulted the profession. The idea: digital-first is the correct default engagement model for 100% of your customers in 2026.

Not digital-only. Digital-first. That distinction is critical and doing a lot of work. The instinct to immediately soften this statement is exactly the problem that keeps CS organizations from building the engagement model the moment actually requires.

The Human Premium: When human interaction happens at every touchpoint regardless of need, it becomes expected and unremarkable. When it happens at the moments that genuinely require human judgment, it becomes memorable and relationship-defining.

Segment 2 · The Real Reason

Why Customers Demand
Humans (Hint: It's Not Preference)

Customers demand human access primarily because the digital experiences they've been given are terrible — not because they prefer relationships over efficiency.

What They've Been Given
Documentation written by engineers for engineers
Tooltips that appear once, never again
17-click support portals before a human responds
Search that returns the wrong 40 of 800 articles
What They Actually Want
Something that actually works
Available at 2am without scheduling
Faster than any human response time
Personalized to their specific use case

The generational shift: Millennials and Gen Z are now in decision-making roles. They research independently, prefer self-service when it works, and find mandatory monthly calls inefficient at best. They're not wrong. Build excellent digital and they'll choose it — not because you told them to, but because it's better.

Segment 3 · The Model

Digital-First: How It
Actually Works

AI-mediated interaction is the default channel. Human engagement is earned by complexity and triggered by signal — not assigned by contract tier.

Gets Human Attention Fast
$20K SMB — Stage 4 Crisis
Champion departure confirmed. Competitive evaluation beginning. The signal demands human attention — not the contract value.
Stays in Digital Layer
$200K Enterprise — Steady State
Full adoption. Stable health score. No signals. A mandatory monthly call here is a Negative Touchpoint — consuming CSM time and customer attention for zero value.

Signal complexity determines when humans engage. Contract value determines the budget. They are not the same thing and should not be treated as the same thing.

Segment 4 · The Human Premium

The Human Premium:
Scarcity Creates Value

When Human = Commodity
Monthly calls regardless of need. Quarterly QBR theater. Scheduled check-ins on no particular signal.

Human interaction becomes expected and unremarkable. The customer learns to sit through it and forget it. Your best CSMs' time and judgment deployed on work that doesn't require either.
When Human = Premium
Champion just departed. New executive needs to understand why they should keep this product. Competitor actively pitching — outcome uncertain.

The customer feels the difference between a CSM showing up because they noticed something important — and a CSM showing up because it's Tuesday.

Scarcity creates value. Your most scarce resource is the judgment and relationship capital of your best Outcome Architects. Deploy it where it generates the highest return.

Segment 5 · The Transition

The 4-Quarter
Transition Plan

Q1
Audit
Catalog every touchpoint. Classify signal-triggered vs. calendar-triggered. Identify which can move digital-first without quality loss. Establish baseline NRR by segment.
Q2
Pilot
One SMB or mid-market segment. Shift to digital-first. Tell customers directly: "We're improving how we deliver value — you'll hear from us when you need us, not on a schedule."
Q3
Scale
Positive pilot data → expand to additional segments. Keep highest-complexity enterprise on hybrid. Document what triggers human escalation by segment.
Q4
Redefine
With digital foundation in place: fewer transactional CSMs, more Outcome Architects, stronger digital experience team. Comp aligned to NRR. Career paths visible. The Human Premium is now real.
Segment 5 · Action

Do This Monday

  • 1
    Run the touchpoint audit on your top 10 accounts. For each scheduled recurring touchpoint — ask: would this fire if it were signal-triggered instead of calendar-triggered? If the answer is no on three or more accounts, those are Negative Touchpoints consuming CSM time that generates no value.
  • 2
    Identify your lowest-volume, most transactional customer segment. That's your Q2 digital-first pilot candidate. Write the message you'd send them: "We're improving how we deliver value — you'll hear from us when you need us, not on a schedule." If that message sounds alarming to you, that's data about how your current model has set expectations.
  • 3
    Count how many hours your CS team spends per week on touchpoints that the digital layer should own. That number is the capacity argument for digital-first — and the CFO argument you'll need in Phase 3. Write it down now so you have a baseline.
