Segment 3 · Structural Diagnosis

3 Reasons the QBR Format Is Broken

  • 1
    Backward-looking by design. 90% of a standard QBR is a recap of data your customer already knows. You're narrating their own data back to them.
  • 2
    Calendar-driven, not signal-driven. Runs on a 90-day schedule with no connection to what's actually happening in the customer's business.
  • 3
    Incentivizes slide volume, not insight. To fill 60 minutes, CSMs bring slides. Slides are not insight. Your customers know the difference.
Segment 4 · The Value Proof Conversation

What Your Executive Customer
Actually Wants

When they give you 25 minutes of their calendar, they want three things:

One specific result you delivered

Connected to something they care about

With one clear recommendation they can act on

The QBR doesn't give them that. The Value Proof Conversation does.

Segment 4 · The 25-Minute Formula

The VPC Formula

0–3 min
Open With Outcome
Name one specific documented business result — not a recap. That's it. You've earned the next 22 minutes.
3–8 min
Connect to Their Business
Link the outcome explicitly to their stated objective. Pull it from your joint success plan.
8–18 min
One Forward Recommendation
Executive-level. Actionable in 30 days. Something they cannot get from their dashboard.
18–23 min
Listen — Don't Fill Silence
Ask: "What is the most important thing on your plate next quarter?" Then stop talking.
23–25 min
Close With a Commitment
One agreed next step. If you leave without a commitment, you had a pleasant conversation — not a VPC.
