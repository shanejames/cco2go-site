Segment 3 · Be Prepared

The 5 CFO Questions —
and How to Answer Each One

  • 1
    What does it cost us when a customer churns?
    CAC to replace + revenue gap during replacement cycle. A churned $50K account costs $200K–$350K to replace. That number changes the conversation.
  • 2
    What does a save play intervention cost?
    $240–$540 per play (8–12 CSM hours). Compare to CAC of $20K–$120K per account. The ROI math is immediately visible.
  • 3
    What does the save play system return?
    Accounts saved × avg ARR ÷ CS save play cost. Target: 10:1 or better. If below 5:1 — check your CAC, close rate, or account qualification.
  • 4
    What does expansion cost vs. acquisition?
    3–5× cheaper per dollar of ARR. This is the investment argument in one number. Calculate it from your own data before the meeting.
  • 5
    What's our NRR trajectory and what moves it?
    Current NRR. 4-quarter trend. Projected at current trajectory. Projected with CS investment. Express the delta as a dollar amount at Year 3.
Segment 4 · Execution

Running the Room: 3 Moves,
3 Mistakes

Make These 3 Moves
1. Lead with the NRR compounding table. Frame before numbers.

2. State your cost per retained ARR dollar before being asked. Don't wait for the question.

3. Make The Ask specific, bounded, timed. "Two hires at $280K. Projected return: 3.0× year one. Reported quarterly." That's a capital deployment request — not a budget ask.
Avoid These 3 Mistakes
1. Leading with CSAT. Signals CS thinks of itself as a service function.

2. Presenting cost without return. That's a budget conversation, not an investment one. CFOs fund investments. They negotiate budgets.

3. Vague timelines. "Next few quarters" = no accountability hook = no reason to believe the return is real.
Segment 5 · The Leave-Behind

The 5-Section Investment
Case One-Pager

1
Current State
ARR base. Current NRR. CS cost as % of ARR. The three input numbers everything else derives from.
2
Cost of Inaction
NRR at current trajectory, Year 3, expressed in dollars. The NRR compounding argument applied to your specific numbers.
3
The Investment
Specific request. Headcount, tooling, or both. Fully-loaded annual cost. No ranges.
4
Projected Return
Churn prevented + expansion sourced = total return. ROI multiple. Year 1, Year 2.
5 — The Accountability Mechanism
How you'll report return quarterly. What metrics. What baseline. When you'll declare success or not.
Almost nobody offers this unprompted. When you do, you've demonstrated that you're thinking about this as a capital deployment decision — not a resourcing request. That's the conversation CFOs want to have.

The 6-Stage Onboarding Waterfall

1
Kickoff → First Login
≤2d SMB · ≤5d MM · ≤10d Ent
Drop: provisioning friction. Fix: CSM-triggered login prompt at Hour 24 if no activity.
2
First Login → First Feature Use
≤3d SMB · ≤7d MM · ≤14d Ent
Drop: first session with no meaningful action. Fix: guided first-use flow + CSM-visible progress dashboard.
3
First Feature Use → Core Workflow Built ← HIGHEST LEAK
≤7d SMB · ≤21d MM · ≤45d Ent
Drop: product adopted as tool, not system. Fix: template library + workflow review call triggered Day 5 post-activation.
4
Core Workflow → Multi-User Adoption
≤21d SMB · ≤45d MM · ≤90d Ent
Drop: champion-only plateau. Fix: expansion champion pack delivered at Stage 3 completion.
5
Multi-User → Business Outcome Documented ← MOST SKIPPED
≤30d SMB · ≤60d MM · ≤120d Ent
Drop: no formalized value story. Fix: CSM-led Outcome Mapping session at Day 20 post-multi-user adoption.
6
Business Outcome → Expansion Signal ← Expansion Staircase begins here
≤90d SMB · ≤120d MM · ≤180d Ent
Stage 5 must be complete or Stage 6 cannot fire. Stage 6 completion rate = expansion flywheel entry rate.
