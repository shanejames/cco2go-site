Segments 2 + 3 · Predictions 1–3

Predictions 1, 2, and 3

  • 1
    By 2030
    AI-native platforms displace today's incumbents
    Not a feature gap — an architectural gap that compounds. Built for CSMs who check dashboards vs. built for agents that act autonomously. Evaluation question: AI core to architecture, or added to it?
    Now: ask every vendor the build-vs-bolt question. More informative than any feature demo.
  • 2
    By 2028
    CRO roles increasingly from post-sales and CS backgrounds
    CRO mandate evolving from new-logo acquisition to full revenue lifecycle. NRR ownership requires post-sales expertise. CS leaders with Module 7.3 financial fluency are the natural candidates.
    Now: financial fluency is not optional career preparation — it's the preparation for this transition.
  • 3
    By 2029
    NRR tied to CEO/CFO compensation, not just CS
    As NRR compounding becomes standard board literacy, it will join ARR growth as executive compensation metric. Accountability for NRR moves to the top of the organization.
    Now: boards learning to read CS investment cases today are the ones making this change.
Segment 4 · Predictions 4–6

Predictions 4, 5, and 6

  • 4
    By 2028
    'CSM' replaced by 'Outcome Architect' at leading companies
    Title reflects mandate. Service orientation → value creation orientation. Different talent, different performance measurement, different competitive advantage.
    Now: Module 6.2 Outcome Architect operating model is the job description being written at leading orgs today.
  • 5
    By 2030
    Agentic AI handles 80% of routine interactions autonomously
    Module 8.2 is the early state. Human CS concentrates in executive relationships, multi-stakeholder expansion, high-judgment situations. The Human Premium argument as a prediction.

    CS and PLG distinction disappears entirely
    Unified motion: product-led for Waterfall Stages 1–3, agents for monitoring, human CS for Stage 5–6. The separation between "product-led" and "success-led" becomes architectural, not organizational.
    Now: you're building the unified motion already through digital-first model, agent architecture, Expansion Staircase.

Segment 3 · The Manifesto

The 5 Commitments

  • 1
    We measure what actually predicts, not what's easy to report.
    We retire vanity metrics the moment they stop predicting — regardless of how long we've tracked them.
  • 2
    We intervene at Stage 2. Not Stage 4.
    The signals were available at Stage 2. We do not wait for the at-risk conversation to begin at Stage 4 because intervention is most effective and least expensive early.
  • 3
    We build expansion from value delivered. Not from product features.
    Stage 5 of the Onboarding Waterfall must be complete before the expansion conversation begins. Expansion without documented business outcome is commercial push without earned foundation.
  • 4
    We speak the CFO's language. Not the CS team's language.
    We know our cost per retained-and-grown ARR dollar. We know the NRR compounding delta. We make The Ask with a specific number, return, and accountability mechanism — before being asked to justify ourselves.
  • 5
    We build systems that outlast us.
    The measure of an Outcome Architect is not how much they personally retain and grow. It's how much the system they built retains and grows after they've moved to the next challenge.
