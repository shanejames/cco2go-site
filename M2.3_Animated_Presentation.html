How to Assign Tiers

3 Inputs. All Required.

INPUT 01
ARR
Objective, pullable, forces an honest conversation about where revenue is actually concentrated. Map it before you segment — you may be surprised.
INPUT 02
Strategic Value
Lighthouse customer? Reference account? Growing 300% YoY? A smaller ARR account with explosive growth potential may outrank a flat large account.
INPUT 03
Churn Risk
Health score deterioration, dropping usage, champion departure — these can temporarily elevate an account's tier regardless of ARR.
Review and adjust tier assignments quarterly. Segmentation is not permanent.

The Tier 3 Misconception

Digital-Led Is Not
Abandonment

Most Tier 3 customers don't want twelve touchpoints a year. They want the product to work and questions answered. Design an engagement model that delivers exactly that — at scale.
Tier 3 isn't a lesser service. It's a different service — designed for what those customers actually need.

When the System Breaks Down

4 Thin Coverage
Warning Signs

1
Last proactive outreach was 60+ days ago. Not a support response — the last time you initiated contact with something non-reactive.
2
Only one named contact. Single-threading is thin coverage by definition. One relationship isn't coverage — it's exposure.
3
You don't know what success looks like today. Not at onboarding. Right now. If your success definition is 6 months old, you may be solving for the wrong thing.
4
You're being surprised by account activity. Renewal shock, unexpected escalations, champion departures as news — if it surprises you, you're not close enough.

Fixing Thin Coverage

Triage First.
Don't Fix Everything at Once.

Thin coverage is a lagging indicator of future churn. The accounts you're not close to today are the accounts that will surprise you at renewal.
Start with Tier 1 and Tier 2 accounts showing health deterioration. Discovery call. Stakeholder map. Sponsor re-engaged. In that order.
