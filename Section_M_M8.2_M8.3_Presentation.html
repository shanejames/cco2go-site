Segment 1 · The Core Distinction

Tool-First vs.
Agent-First Architecture

Tool-First (Most CS Platforms Today)
CSM logs in. Checks dashboards. Pulls reports. Interprets data. Decides what to do.

AI, if it exists, is a feature inside the tool — suggested action buried in a menu, score that updates when someone remembers to refresh it.

The CSM is checking the system.
Agent-First (What We're Building)
Agents run continuously in the background. CSMs receive briefings, alerts, recommended plays, CSQL assignments — when agents detect something that warrants attention.

The CSM is not checking the system.
The system is notifying the CSM.

That inversion is architectural, not feature-level.

Segment 3 · Highest-Impact Agents

The 4 Implementation Priorities

  • Briefing Agent
    Trigger: meeting in calendar system
    Highest immediate ROI
    Generates account briefing from CRM, support history, health score, sentiment. Delivered 24 hours before meeting. Eliminates 3–5 hours of weekly meeting prep per CSM. Quality = quality of Layer 1 data.
  • Champion Departure Monitor
    Trigger: title change OR 21-day silence
    Most time-sensitive
    Monitors LinkedIn + email patterns. Fires 48-Hour Departure Protocol automatically. Detection must be within 24 hours — the window from departure to "account stabilized" closes fast. Manual check-ins miss it.
  • CSQL Qualification Agent
    Trigger: Staircase Step 3 OR expansion feature spike
    Expansion scale mechanism
    Evaluates every account against CSQL criteria continuously. Without it: expansion identified by CSMs who happen to notice. With it: pipeline of opportunities that didn't exist last month.
  • Churn Prediction Agent
    Trigger: weekly cadence, churn prob >65% at T-90
    Weights combinations, not individual signals
    Scores renewal probability using historical churn patterns. Fires save play assignment + CSM + manager notification. Requires health scoring foundation from Month 1 to function well.

Segment 5 · The Missing Design

The Human Escalation Layer

Agents without escalation design produce alert fatigue. CSMs learn alerts have low urgency. Signals get ignored. The account reaches Stage 4 before anyone acts.

1
Agent fires → CSM alert delivered
Specific action requested. Score breakdown + recommended play. Clock starts.
2
48 hours — no action logged → manager escalation
Manager receives notification with account details. CSM capacity issue surfaced, not buried.
3
72 hours — no action → automated play initiation
System initiates standard Stage response. Account is not waiting on human availability.

Escalation ladder also solves coverage gaps: CSM vacation, weekend, competing priorities. For enterprise accounts at renewal risk, a 48-hour response delay can be the difference between a save and a churn.

Segment 2 · The Structure

Anatomy of a Living Playbook

1
Signal-Triggered Entry
Playbook starts when a behavioral signal crosses a threshold — not when a CSM decides to run it. Stage 2 Cascade confirmed. Champion departure detected. T-90 window opened.

Signal is the starting gun. Not CSM memory.
2
Defined Workflow Sequence
Not "reach out to the customer." Specific action, specific timeline, specific responsibility, specific success criteria at each step.

"CSM initiates discovery call within 48 hours using Stage 2 talk track, goal: identify objection category." No ambiguity about what next step means.
3
Outcome Feedback Loop
Every playbook run logs outcome, talk track used, objection raised, CSM, time-to-resolution. Over time, playbook content updates based on that data.

Top-performer behavior propagates automatically — not through training sessions six months later.

This is what most orgs are missing.

Without Component 3 (the feedback loop), the playbook stops improving the day it's written. Most CS organizations have Components 1 and 2 in some form. Almost none have 3.

Segment 3 · The Playbook Library

The 6 Core Playbooks

  • Save Play
    Churn Cascade Stage 2 confirmed by Churn Prediction Agent
    Stage-specific actions: Stage 2 = discovery call. Stage 3 = champion escalation. Stage 4 = exec sponsor. Stage 5 = custom ROI build. Stage 2 and Stage 5 are not the same conversation.
    Recovery rate by stage: S2 target >70%, S3 >50%, S4 >30%
  • Renewal Preparation
    T-90 trigger from Renewal Preparation Agent
    T-90: briefing + VPC scheduled. T-60: VPC executed, expansion tabled. T-45: commercial initiated. T-30: contract review. T-14: final risk check. These are workflow triggers, not calendar suggestions. System enforces the timeline.
    Target: >80% of renewals with VPC executed at T-60 or earlier
  • Champion Departure
    Champion Departure Monitor fires 48-Hour Protocol
    Hour 0: CSM alerted. Hour 2: relationship map updated. Hour 24: internal sponsor identified. Hour 48: outreach to new contact. Week 2: exec sponsor if needed.
    Success: 2+ contacts engaged within 30 days of departure
  • Expansion Staircase
    CSQL Qualification Agent creates CSQL
    Step-specific: Step 1 = value gap identification. Step 2 = expansion use case demo. Step 3 = ROI build. Step 4 = commercial. Step 5 = contract.
    CSQL-to-expansion ARR conversion target: >35%
  • Onboarding Waterfall
    Stage milestone missed >2× SLA
    Automated: milestone reminder + help content. CSM: workflow review call triggered. Manager: escalation if still blocked at next checkpoint.
    Days to next stage vs. benchmark SLA
  • Competitive Response
    Competitive Signal Agent detects evaluation language
    Hour 0: CSM alert + competitive response playbook surfaced. Day 2: champion call. Day 7: ROI comparison delivered. Day 14: exec sponsor if needed.
    Evaluation win rate target: >60% when intervention at Stage 1
Segments 4 + 5 · Build It Right

Feedback Loop + Build Order

Building the Feedback Loop
3-question outcome form embedded in CRM at play close: outcome, primary objection, what worked.

2 minutes. Over 50 plays = patterns. Over 100 = predictive model foundation.

One CSM at 80% recovery rate vs. 50% team avg: system identifies pattern → content extracted → next playbook version incorporates it. Without feedback loop: stays in their head.
Build Order
  • 1
    Save Play — highest ROI, clearest trigger. Stages 2+3 first.
  • 2
    Renewal Prep — highest-stakes scheduled event. Before next renewal cycle.
  • 3
    Champion Departure — 48-hour window can't be hit manually at scale.
  • +
    Expansion Staircase, Onboarding Waterfall, Competitive Response — in order of current priority.

The playbook is how every CSM executes at the level of your best performer — not your average one. Build the playbooks.
