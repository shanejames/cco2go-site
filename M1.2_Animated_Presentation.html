Why NRR Gets Inflated
The NRR
Shell Game
01 ‚Äî The Price Increase Rebrand
An 8% price increase shows up as "expansion" ‚Äî even when customers aren't buying more or using more. Strip this out and most companies' NRR drops 5‚Äì8 points immediately.
02 ‚Äî The Forced Bundle
A new product gets bundled into renewal contracts. Customers say yes because unbundling is more work than accepting. Revenue goes up ‚Äî but the customer didn't choose to expand.
03 ‚Äî The Rebase
An account drops from 100 seats to 60, then adds 10 back. The 10 seats are counted as "expansion" ‚Äî the 40 that left are ignored. Counting additions without subtractions is how fiction becomes a board metric.

The 5-Step NRR Truth Test
Build Your
True NRR
1
Strip Out Price Increases
Remove revenue from contractual price escalators. If organic NRR drops 5+ points, you have a pricing problem masquerading as a growth story.
2
Audit Your Expansion Deals
For each positive-expansion account: did the customer actively choose to buy more, or did revenue increase through a mechanism they didn't initiate? >30% passive = inflated NRR.
3
Net Contraction in the Same Period
Account A adds 20 seats. Account B loses 15. Net expansion is 5, not 20. Run expansion and contraction together ‚Äî never separately.
4
Cohort NRR, Not Aggregate
110% aggregate may hide Enterprise at 130% and SMB at 75%. Break it down by segment and cohort ‚Äî that's where the real story lives.
5
Calculate Your True NRR
After stripping price increases, forced bundles, and rebased calculations ‚Äî this is your number. The starting point for every strategic decision.
