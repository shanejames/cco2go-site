Segment 3 · Process 2.0

Living Playbooks
vs. PDF Graveyards

Most CS playbooks describe how things worked when they were written. They have no mechanism for learning from what's working now. That's the PDF graveyard.

Layer 1
Signal
Every play starts with a behavioral trigger — not a calendar date. Usage decline, NPS drop, champion departure, expansion signal. The play doesn't exist until the signal fires.
Layer 2
Prediction
Signal feeds a model trained on historical outcomes. CSM gets a prioritized action recommendation — not a raw data point to interpret manually.
Layer 3
Workflow
Prediction triggers action: save play assigned, CSM briefed with context, meeting scheduled, executive alerted if escalation criteria met. Before the CSM has to consciously decide what to do next.
Segment 4 · Technology 2.0

The 3-Layer
Signal-to-Action Stack

Layer 1
Data Foundation
Behavioral signals, usage patterns, financial data, support data. Most important layer. Most under-invested.
Activity data ≠ behavioral data. Build Layer 1 on the wrong signals and every prediction above it is confidently wrong.
Layer 2
Predictive Models
Signals weighted against historical outcomes. Portfolio-scale risk and opportunity scoring simultaneously. This is where AI earns its value.
Layer 3
Automated Workflows
Predictions trigger actions: churn risk → save play, expansion signal → CSQL, renewal approaching → preparation sequence. No one has to remember. The system remembers.

Build in order. Teams that skip to Layer 3 without Layer 1 quality produce systems CSMs stop trusting — and you cannot rebuild that trust cheaply.
