Segment 1 · Cold Open

The Most Important Question
Nobody Asks

A CSM notices heavy usage. Multiple teams involved. Great signal. And then — because there's no framework — they wait, mention it in Slack, or walk into a renewal with a vague "we also have this other thing you might like."

None of those work. Not because the opportunity wasn't real. Because the approach ignored the most important question: What step is this customer on?

Expansion is not a single conversation. It's a staircase. Skipping steps doesn't accelerate anything. It collapses the whole structure.

Segment 3 · Step 2

Value Expansion

Signal
Feature ceiling approach — heavy utilization, workarounds appearing in support, requests for capabilities they don't have
Trigger
Use Case Extension: "You've been using the analytics module at full capacity for three months. Companies at your stage typically hit this point when they're ready to add the predictive layer — here's what that looks like for an account like yours."
Goal
Extend scope within the existing product. The customer doesn't feel sold — they feel understood.
Failure mode
Skipping to Step 3 before Value Expansion is complete. Step 2 is the value case for everything above it.
Segment 3 · Step 3

Seat Expansion

Signal
Cross-departmental friction — someone outside primary team needs platform data, manager pulling reports manually, growth hiring in relevant function
Trigger
Team Expansion: "Your ops team is working around the data every week — what would it mean for their output if they had direct access instead of requesting it from you?"
Goal
Make the value case without making the price case. Let them make the price case themselves.
Failure mode
Leading with user count pricing. Don't. Lead with the friction. License conversation follows naturally.
Segment 4 · Step 4

Tier Upgrade

Signal
Recurring advanced feature requests — same capability across multiple conversations, quarters, and stakeholders. Not one ask. A pattern.
3 specifics
Reporting/compliance needs emerging · Executive visibility desired · Integration requirements growing
Trigger
Capability Unlock: "You've asked about advanced reporting three separate times. That's a Tier 2 feature — let me show you exactly what it does for a company at your stage before we talk about the upgrade."
Rule
Show the value first. Show the price second. Always.
Segment 4 · Step 5

Strategic Partnership

Who gets here
Most accounts will never reach Step 5. The ones that do are your most valuable, most loyal, and most referenceable customers.
Signal
Executive sponsor referencing your platform in their own planning conversations — asking about your product roadmap because they want to align their business to it
Trigger
Partnership Frame: "Given where your business is going in the next 18 months — I don't think we should be thinking about this as a vendor relationship anymore. I think we should be planning this together. What would a 3-year roadmap conversation look like?"
The shift
At Step 5, the conversation isn't about price. It's about alignment. The relationship is genuinely mutual.

Segment 5 · Where Teams Break Down

The Two Breakdown Points

The staircase doesn't fail because the customer wasn't ready. It fails because of execution at two predictable points.

Breakdown #1
Skipping Step 2
Full Adoption confirmed → immediately push for Seat Expansion or Tier Upgrade. Customer resists because the value case hasn't been built. Step 2 is the value case for everything above it. Skip it = asking for money before proving ROI.
Breakdown #2
Wrong Conversation at Right Step
At Step 3, leading with user count pricing. At Step 4, describing features instead of outcomes. Signal is right. Timing is right. Framing is wrong. Right moment, wrong language = lost opportunity that won't reopen easily.

The CSM either moved too fast — or spoke the wrong language at the right moment. Both are fixable. Both require the same thing: a framework.

Segment 6 · Action

Do This Monday

  • 1
    Map every Tier 1 account to their current Expansion Staircase step. Not where you want them — where the signals say they actually are. If you don't know the step for any Tier 1 account — you have a visibility problem, not an expansion problem.
  • 2
    For every account on Step 2 or above — verify Full Adoption (≥70%) is confirmed first. If below 70% → remove from expansion queue → move to value delivery queue. Wrong order accelerates churn, not revenue.
  • 3
    For accounts legitimately on Step 2 or 3 — identify the one signal that advances them to the next step. Build a 30-day plan to create that condition. Not to close the deal — to create the signal. The deal follows the signal. Always.
