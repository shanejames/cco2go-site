Segment 3 · The Structure

The CSQL: Post-Sale MQL

The Customer Success Qualified Lead — expansion opportunity identified by CS based on account signals, conversation data, and usage behavior. Companies spend enormous resources on MQL rigor. Most have no equivalent for CSQLs.

Signal Identification
Usage growth at ceiling, new stakeholders, budget cycle timing, feature adoption capacity
Qualification Standards
3 criteria that make it a CSQL vs. a wish. Write it down. If three people give three answers — you have opinions, not a process.
48-Hour SLA
Every CSQL gets a response within 48 hours. Buying windows close fast. CSQLs sitting untouched for a week lose momentum.
Measurement
Volume, conversion rate, average deal size, time to close — tracked exactly like any pipeline metric.
Segment 4 · The Framing Error

The Fatal Framing Error

✗ Triggers Procurement
"We have a new feature we'd like to tell you about."
✓ Opens a Conversation
"Based on your usage patterns, you're generating $400K in annual value. Companies with similar profiles see an additional $200K from the advanced analytics module. Given your Q3 cycle, would it make sense to explore this?"

You're not asking for money. You're offering insight. Not selling — advising. That framing changes everything.

Segment 5 · The 70% Rule

The Adoption Gate

If your customer is using 40% of the features they're paying for and you propose expansion — you haven't failed the expansion conversation. You've confirmed the current product isn't delivering value. The most effective argument for churn.
